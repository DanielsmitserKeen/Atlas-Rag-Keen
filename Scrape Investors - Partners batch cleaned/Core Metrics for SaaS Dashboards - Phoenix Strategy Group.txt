## Financial Metrics to Track

Tracking the right financial metrics is crucial for growing and maintaining a profitable SaaS business. Here’s a breakdown of the key metrics to monitor:

### MRR and ARR Tracking

Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) are two of the most important indicators for SaaS performance. MRR gives you a snapshot of monthly progress, while ARR helps with long-term planning and discussions with investors.

| **Revenue Metric** | **What to Monitor** | **Why It Matters** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MRR Growth Rate | Month-over-month change | Reflects short-term business trajectory |
| New MRR | Revenue from new customers | Measures success in acquiring customers |
| Expansion MRR | Revenue from upgrades | Shows how effective upselling efforts are |
| Churned MRR | Revenue lost from cancellations | Highlights retention challenges |

> "MRR, or Monthly Recurring Revenue, is the total predictable revenue your SaaS business generates each month from subscription-based customers." - Kalungi

Next, let’s look at customer acquisition costs and the value customers bring over time.

### CAC and CLV Analysis

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) help gauge efficiency and sustainability. Ideally, the LTV/CAC ratio should be around 3:1 for a healthy balance between growth and profitability.

| **Ratio Range** | **Business Implication** |
| --- | --- |
| LTV/CAC < 1.0x | Unsustainable – acquisition costs outweigh customer value |
| LTV/CAC = 3.0x | Balanced – indicates efficient growth |
| LTV/CAC > 5.0x | May signal underinvestment in growth opportunities |

While acquisition is important, keeping an eye on churn is just as critical for long-term success.

### Understanding Churn

Churn directly affects profitability. Even a small reduction in churn - say 5% - can increase profitability by up to 95% over five years. That’s why tracking both customer churn and revenue churn is essential.

| **Metric** | **Target Range** | **Action Trigger** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Annual Churn Rate | 5-7% | Above 7% requires immediate attention |
| Monthly Revenue Churn | 0.42-0.58% | Over 1% may indicate pricing or value issues |
| Net Revenue Retention | Over 100% | Below 100% shows negative growth trends |

To stay ahead, monitor payment success rates, use dunning management to recover failed payments, and track service usage to identify customers at risk of leaving. These actions can help you address potential problems before they escalate.

## User Metrics to Monitor

Financial metrics might show revenue trends, but user metrics tell the story of engagement and satisfaction - key elements for long-term SaaS success. Understanding how users interact with your product helps you identify what’s working and what needs attention.

### Active User Numbers

Tracking **Daily Active Users (DAU)** and **Monthly Active Users (MAU)** is essential to understand how "sticky" your product is. The **DAU/MAU ratio**, also called the "stickiness ratio", reveals how often users return. By analyzing activity patterns, you can spot peak engagement times and identify when users start dropping off.

### Customer Retention

Customer retention plays a huge role in maintaining steady revenue and driving growth. For SaaS, a retention rate above 85% is often seen as a strong benchmark, especially for B2B companies, which tend to perform better than B2C services. To calculate retention, use this formula:  
**CRR = ((E - N) / S) x 100**

*   **E**: Customers at the end of the period
*   **N**: New customers acquired
*   **S**: Customers at the start of the period

### NPS Measurement

The **Net Promoter Score (NPS)** is a powerful way to measure customer satisfaction and loyalty. It helps predict churn and highlights areas needing improvement. For instance, Slack’s NPS of 55 (68% promoters, 19% passives, 13% detractors) reflects its focus on delivering a great user experience. Conducting regular NPS surveys and analyzing feedback can uncover pain points and guide product updates. Dropbox, for example, has used NPS insights to shape its product strategy effectively.

## Dashboard Setup Guide

Creating a SaaS metrics dashboard helps bring your key metrics into focus, making it easier to make quick, informed decisions. To build a dashboard that works, you’ll need thoughtful planning and smart design.

### Design Best Practices

Design your dashboard with the end user in mind. Stephen Few, a well-known expert in data visualization, explains it best: "A dashboard is a visual display of the most important information needed to achieve one or more objectives; consolidated and arranged on a single screen so the information can be monitored at a glance."

Here are some tips to guide your design:

*   **Visual Hierarchy**: Highlight the most critical metrics by placing them at the top. Use size and color to show their importance.
*   **Data Density**: Find the right balance between showing enough data and keeping the dashboard easy to understand.
*   **Color Consistency**: Stick to a clear color scheme - like red for problems and green for positive trends.
*   **White Space**: Leave enough space between elements to make the dashboard easier to read.

Noah Iliinsky and Julie Steele emphasize the importance of simplicity: "When reading a visualization (or any other kind of communication), your reader has a limited amount of brainpower to dedicate to the problem...any brainpower that is left may then be used to understand the message (if the reader hasn't yet given up in frustration)." In short, keep your design clear and intuitive to avoid overwhelming users.

### Audience-Specific Views

Tailor dashboards to meet the needs of different teams by focusing on metrics that matter most to them.

| Role | Primary Metrics | Update Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Executives | MRR, CAC, CLV | Daily |
| Finance Teams | Revenue, Expenses, Cash Flow | Real-time |
| Product Managers | User Engagement, Feature Usage | Hourly |
| Sales Teams | Pipeline, Conversion Rates | Real-time |

### Live Data Updates

Timely access to data is essential for making informed decisions. Dashboards should deliver updates almost instantly while staying fast and responsive. Studies show that users, especially on mobile, often abandon tools that lag.

To boost performance:

*   Use real-time data engines capable of processing complex queries quickly.
*   Pre-aggregate data to speed up results.
*   Set up streaming data pipelines for continuous updates.
*   Ensure the dashboard is mobile-friendly for easy access on the go.

> "Including too many metrics, charts, or information can overwhelm users and dilute the dashboard's effectiveness." - Data Visualist

Focus on real-time updates for the metrics that matter most, so your team can act without delay.
